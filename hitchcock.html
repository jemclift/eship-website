"Unexpectedly brilliant – suspense with showtunes!"
⭐️9.3
– Chris
I wasn't sure what to expect from a musical based on Hitchcock, but this completely blew me away. It somehow managed to blend suspense, comedy, and catchy tunes with eerie precision. The shadowy lighting and minimalist set design built such an atmospheric world, and the cast leaned into the campiness with just the right amount of theatrical flair. The “Psycho” tap number? Genius. Completely unique and one of the most memorable shows I’ve seen this year.
"Campy, clever, and so stylish"
⭐️7.0
– Oliwer
This show totally nails the tone – it’s got that vintage, noir feel but also winks at the audience constantly. The music had serious Sondheim vibes in places, and the cast handled the intricate harmonies beautifully. Every little nod to classic Hitchcock moments felt intentional and earned, especially during “Dial M For Medley.” If you like musicals with a bit of edge and a lot of wit, don’t miss this one.
"The most original show I’ve seen in ages"
⭐️9.6
– Adi
Hitchcock: The Musical is just insanely creative. The writing, the direction, the staging – it all came together in this strange but captivating way. One minute you’re laughing at a cheeky Vertigo reference, the next you’re genuinely feeling tense during a silent ensemble piece that builds to a huge musical payoff. I kept thinking, “How is this even a real production?” The cast clearly loved what they were doing and the audience was right there with them the whole time.
"A darkly questionable masterpiece"
⭐️6.1
– Jem
This show was something else. The way it combined the tension of Hitchcock thrillers with the theatricality of musical numbers was so unexpected, and okayly executed. The performances were committed and magnetic, yet and the musical motifs woven throughout failed to keep you hooked. Costumes and lighting were simple – but it let the actors and the story take the spotlight. Honestly, I walked in expecting a fun parody, but left feeling like I’d just seen something genuinely okay.
Audience Reviews
“Suspense, style, and songs — a killer combination.”
⭐️9.6
– Isabelle F.
Who knew Hitchcock could be turned into a musical — and such a *good* one at that? Every number oozed suspense and theatrical flair, with cheeky nods to classic films like *Psycho* and *Rear Window*. The cast was outstanding, and the direction was tight, clever, and so fun to watch.
“More thrilling than half the West End.”
⭐️9.8
– Oliver K.
Honestly? This production had no right to be this good. The writing was sharp, the songs were memorable, and the suspense was brilliantly balanced with tongue-in-cheek humour. The audience was howling one minute and gasping the next. If you ever get the chance — go see it.
“Perfectly camp, perfectly eerie, perfectly Hitchcock.”
⭐️9.5
– Tamzin L.
It’s like Hitchcock directed *Chicago* with a dash of Sondheim. The tone walked that tightrope between thrilling and theatrical so well. The music was cleverly written, with eerie motifs that stuck in your head. Huge kudos to the lighting and sound team — total chills during some scenes!
“A masterclass in mood and melody.”
⭐️9.7
– Marcus D.
One of the most inventive productions I’ve seen in years. From the slashing violins in the overture to the slow build of the final act, the tension was *musically* crafted, which was such a clever twist. Hitchcock would’ve been proud — and maybe a little jealous.
“A thrilling tribute that never takes itself too seriously.”
⭐️9.3
– Ellie M.
The whole thing was a delight — part love letter, part parody, all performed with absolute commitment. The lead actor as Hitchcock’s 'shadow' narrator was genius, and the transitions between scenes were so stylish. You could tell the creative team had fun with this, and so did we.
“Unmissable. An absolute triumph of concept and execution.”
⭐️10.0
– Alex N.
I went in curious — I left completely obsessed. This is one of those rare productions where every department was firing at 110%. Brilliantly acted, flawlessly staged, and musically rich. The standing ovation at the end was instant and well-earned. I’d see it again in a heartbeat.
